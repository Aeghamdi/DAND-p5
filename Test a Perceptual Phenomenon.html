A paired t-test was run on a sample of 24 participants to determine whether there was a statistically significant mean difference between the reaction time between the congruent vs. incongruent condition. Typically it takes participants longer to say the ink colors (22.02 4.8 sec) than it does to read the words (14.05 3.56 sec). At the 95% confidence level ( = .05) and 23 degrees of freedom, the critical statistic value for a one-tailed test is 1.714. The calculated t-value for the difference between the congruent and incongruent conditions is 8.02. The calculated t-value is greater than the critical t-value (t-table value). Additionally, the p-value is less than the alpha level: p < .05; therefore, the null hypothesis is rejected
